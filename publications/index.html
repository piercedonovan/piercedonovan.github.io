Where I am the corresponding author, I have hosted an open-access preprint. If I did the computational work for a project, you’ll also find replication code here.

Visualizing causal hypotheses in environmental econometrics
Pierce Donovan
Review of Environmental Economics and Policy (2024)

This paper explores an explicit framework to help researchers communicate beliefs about how their data is generated and argues why this sort of thing is important for empirical research targeting causal effect estimation.

Keywords: causal models, identification, research design, specification testing, communication, directed acyclic graphs

Balancing conservation and commerce: A shadow value viability approach for governing bycatch
Pierce Donovan and Michael Springborn
Journal of Environmental Economics and Management (2022)

This paper develops a dynamic programming algorithm for addressing a mathematically complex class of endangered species viability objectives and provides a novel non-market valuation approach to inform conservation policy.

Keywords: dynamic programming methods, shadow valuation, avoiding extinction, bycatch, market-based instruments

[ Preprint] [ Code] [ JEEM Article]

Safety in numbers: Cost-effective endangered species management for viable populations
Pierce Donovan, Lucas Bair, Charles Yackulic, and Michael Springborn
Land Economics (2019)

This paper outlines an approach to determine a population abundance target that reduces both the risk and cost of species conservation programs and is informed via models of population dynamics and optimization over available management actions.

Keywords: endangered species, population viability, chance-constrained dynamic programming

Recreational leasing of Alaska commercial Halibut quota: The early years of the GAF program in Alaska
Kailin Kroetz, Daniel Lew, James Sanchirico, and Pierce Donovan
Coastal Management (2019)

This paper studies early charter participation in the Pacific Halibut Catch Sharing Plan, a program that allows the trading of fishing quota between the commercial and recreational charter sectors in Alaska.

Keywords: intersector quota allocation, commercial and recreational fisheries
