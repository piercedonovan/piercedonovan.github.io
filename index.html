About Me

I’m a natural resource economist at the University of Nevada, Reno. I enjoy applying ideas from ecology, mathematics, and physics in my research. Most of my work focuses on the modeling of bioeconomic systems and novel approaches to managing them. I spend equal amounts of time thinking about rivers and optimization algorithms.

This site will tell you about what I’ve been working on recently, so here’s my CV if you want to know about my past work. Feel free to reach out: pierce.donovan@unr.edu

Teaching Schedule (expected to continue for some time):

  • Fall: Applied Econometrics (ECON 741)
  • Spring: Introduction to Econometrics (ECON 441)
  • Occasionally: Natural Resource Economics (ECON 309)

When I’m not at my desk, I like to spend my time backpacking (both the hiking and traveling variants). I recently discovered the joys of bicycle touring for myself.
